Ear

Outer Ear

The outer ear includes the pinna and the ear canal, which collect sound waves and funnel them toward the eardrum.

Middle Ear

The middle ear contains the ossicles, three tiny bones that amplify sound vibrations from the eardrum to the inner ear.

Inner Ear

The inner ear houses the cochlea, which converts vibrations into neural signals, and the vestibular system, which helps maintain balance.

Anatomy

Mouth

Functions

The mouth, or oral cavity, plays essential roles in speaking, eating, and breathing. It contains teeth, tongue, salivary glands, and muscles for these functions.
